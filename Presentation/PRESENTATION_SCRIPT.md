### 1. THE HOOK (0:00 – 0:30) 💡

> *"You're not alone. Every single one of us has done it. And every time we throw that food away, we're not just wasting money — we're heating the planet."*

> *"What if your phone could look inside your fridge… and make sure that never happens again?"*

**[CLICK → Title slide: "FreshSave — AI That Saves Food Before It's Wasted"]**

---

### 2. THE PROBLEM (0:30 – 1:00) 🌍
**[SLIDE: Problem statistics]**

> *"Here's the reality. More than a quarter of all food produced globally is wasted — and food waste alone accounts for 8 to 10 percent of all greenhouse gas emissions."*

> *"In Ireland, households throw away 200 thousand tonnes of food in 2023. That's €60 a month per household — €700 a year — adding up to 1.29 billion euros nationally. According to the EPA."*

> *"The root causes are deceptively simple: we forget what's in our fridge, we don't know when food expires, we don't know what to cook with what we have, and when something goes bad — we have no idea what else to do with it."*

> *"These are human problems. And we built a human-centred AI to solve them."*

---

### 3. HOW IT WORKS (1:00 – 2:35) 🚀
**[SLIDE: Architecture diagram — Input Modes → Smart Pantry → Meal Planner + Exit Engine]**

> *"So, how does FreshSave actually work? Think of it as a three-stage 'brain' for your kitchen.
First, we have the Intake. We knew that if logging food felt like a chore, nobody would do it. So we built four effortless ways to get data in—snap a photo, scan a receipt, use your voice, or a quick manual entry. This builds your Smart Pantry.
Now, the Pantry is where the 'Smart' happens. It's not just a list; it's a live tracker. The AI analyzes every item and categorizes it: Is it Fresh? Do you need to Use it Soon? Or is it reaching a Critical state?
From here, the system takes over. > If the food is good, our Meal Planner steps in. It doesn't just give you random recipes—it prioritizes your 'Use Soon' items first to make sure they actually get eaten.
But our biggest innovation is the Exit Strategy. > We have a rule: We don't just tell you there's an old orange in your fridge—we find the best possible future for that orange before it ever hits a landfill.
We give you three clear paths: Upcycle it into something else, like a face mask or garden compost; Donate it if it's still good; or Dispose of it the right way.
And to keep it safe, we built a Triple-Check System. The engine cross-references the freshness score, the visual appearance, and official safety standards. If it's not 100% safe to eat or share, the AI blocks that path and suggests a better one.
It's about taking the 'guesswork' out of being responsible. Let's see how that looks in the actual app"*

### 4. DEMO (2:35 – 4:10) 📸
**[SLIDE: Demo video plays]**

**[Play the demo video. Narrate over it — keep pace with what's on screen:]**

> *"We snap a photo of our fridge. The AI identifies every item, scores it based on visual signs of freshness, and stores it straight into the pantry."*

> *"From the pantry, we generate meals — and the planner prioritises items that need to be used soon."*

> *"It also factors in our dietary preferences and biometric data from a smartwatch — so meals aren't just smart, they're personal."*

> *"We can save recipes we love, or mark them as cooked. When we do — ingredients are automatically removed from the pantry, and the savings show up on our impact dashboard."*

> *"Here's where saved recipes live. And over here — our nutrition analysis dashboard tells us how balanced our pantry actually is."*

> *"Last but not least — our Exit Strategy engine. Food we don't need doesn't go to waste. It gets a second life — upcycled, donated, or responsibly disposed."*

> *"We can also mark items as used directly from the pantry — and that feeds back into the impact dashboard too."*
